# The Western Lands

A deep dive into the **western hemisphere** of Orogen planet
[`06cy8w6z6a89kow6psje93`](https://www.orogen.studio/#06cy8w6z6a89kow6psje93).

Orogen has four major continents. Two of them sit in the western hemisphere and
were once a *single* landmass — the "west flank" of the planet's last
supercontinent — before it rifted in two and opened an ocean between the halves.
This document treats those two continents and the sea that divides them as one
region: **the Western Lands**.

> **Naming.** *Meridia*, *Selvana*, and the *Western Ocean* are the names used
> throughout the Atlas (`tools/tectonics-pipeline/continents.yaml`,
> `docs/GEOLOGICAL_HISTORY.md`). "**The Western Lands**" is a collective label
> introduced *here* for the two western-hemisphere continents plus their shared
> ocean. Following the repo convention: continent **shapes** are `MEASURED` from
> the export; **craton composition** and the **breakup history** are `INTERPRETED`
> from the reconstructed tectonics; all proper **names** (including "Western
> Lands") are `INVENTED`.

Together: **~55.7 Mkm² of land — more than half the planet's continental
surface — split between the highest, most rugged continent and the lowest, most
fertile one, on opposite sides of a young ocean.**

---

## Meridia — the high west

The planet's **largest and highest** continent (cratons A·I·J, centroid
30°N 105°W). Its defining feature is altitude: a 2.01 km mean and the **tallest
ground on the planet** — the **O5 cordillera** on craton I's western margin,
where ridge subduction and arc pile-up are still driving peaks past **8.4 km**
*today*. The continent's spine is young and active; its interior is dry.

- **Climate.** Arid-dominant (35.6 % Köppen-B) but split three ways: a genuinely
  **tropical north** (24.8 % A), a temperate midriff (15.6 % C), and a
  **polar/alpine** tail (14.7 % E) driven as much by elevation as by latitude.
- **Habitats** (from `BIOGEOGRAPHY.md`): grass/scrub and light forest dominate
  the land; the high spine carries **barren alpine at ~3.6 km** and **glaciers
  above 5 km**, while sheltered lowlands hold **jungle pockets at NPP 2068** —
  one of the most productive habitats anywhere on Orogen, a stone's throw from
  ice.
- **Tectonics.** A **Chilean-type / compressional** margin (5,053 vs 1,703 px):
  Andean cordillera-building along a "Pacific-style" western rim, the legacy of
  the A+J+I weld along the **O1 suture** (700 km of stacked crust) and subduction
  stepping outboard onto the new continent's edge.

## Selvana — the green west

The **lowest** (1.09 km mean) and **most biologically productive** continent
(cratons C·E·F, centroid 22°S 146°W, NPP 1295). Where Meridia is high and dry,
Selvana is low, wet, and green, spanning an enormous latitude range
(roughly 64°S to 27°N).

- **Climate.** The most **tropical** continent (39.9 % Köppen-A) with very little
  polar land (4.3 % E). Warmth and rain, not altitude, set the tone.
- **Habitats.** Its **medium forest** province is the single largest habitat (by
  share of land) of any continent on the planet (~9.9 % of all land), backed by
  a deep **jungle-heavy core at NPP 2023** and broad grass/scrub. Tiny barren,
  desert, and glacier fractions — Selvana is a continent of forests.
- **Tectonics.** A **Mariana-type / extensional** margin overall (2,737 vs
  4,754 px) — the opposite of Meridia — *but* it also carries the long-lived
  **O3 Andean belt** on craton C's southwestern margin, a cordillera that has
  been building continuously and is **still active today**.

## The Western Ocean — the sea between

The two continents face each other across the **Western Ocean**, a basin that is
not incidental but *causal*: Meridia and Selvana are the two halves of a single
ancestral landmass, and the Western Ocean is the rift that split them.

- **Birth.** Opened ~**400 Myr ago** along rift **R3** / the **Western Ridge**,
  tearing the old west flank into the **A-J-I group → Meridia** and the
  **C-E-F group → Selvana**.
- **Today.** The ocean is now old enough to begin consuming itself:
  **intra-oceanic subduction (trench T2, ~[2.5°, -119°])** has started, feeding
  the **micro_7 / O7 island arc**. A relict breakup plume (**L5**) still burns
  beneath plate *ii*, trailing the **H2–H3–H12–H14 hotspot chain** across the
  basin floor.

## Shared deep history (the west flank, 750 Myr)

The Western Lands have a single, legible biography in `GEOLOGICAL_HISTORY.md`:

1. **Assembly.** The A-J group docks against the core along the **S1 western
   suture** — the west flank is welded onto the supercontinent.
2. **Incubation.** Under the supercontinent lid, plume heads pool beneath the
   **western flank**; broad domal uplift, then **L1 flood basalts** mark the
   first magmatic warning of breakup.
3. **Breakup.** Rift **R1** opens the **Central Ocean**, and the whole west flank
   (A, I, J, C, E, F) tears away along the **Central Ridge**.
4. **The west splits in two.** Rift **R3** opens the **Western Ocean**: the
   A-J-I group (→ Meridia) parts from the C-E-F group (→ Selvana). Meridia's
   leading edge turns **Andean**; **L5** is the last breakup flood basalt.
5. **Mountain-building.** A and J close on I and weld into Meridia along **O1**;
   subduction steps to Meridia's western rim. On Selvana, craton C's margin
   settles into the sustained **O3 Andean** regime. Then the modern climax:
   **O5** on Meridia's far-western rim — ridge subduction lifting the planet's
   **highest peaks (8.4 km), still rising**.
6. **Next.** The reconstruction **projects the west to reunite**: Meridia +
   Selvana + Borea coalescing into a **boreal supercontinent in ~200–300 Myr**.
   The Western Lands are a temporary configuration, mid-cycle.

## What we already have

Everything below is derived from the same 2.56-million-cell export; nothing here
is hand-drawn.

**On `main` / this branch:**

- **Raw data** — the full export under `data/orogen_regions_full/` (elevation,
  Köppen, winds, currents, tectonic fields per cell).
- **The 750-Myr geological history** (`docs/GEOLOGICAL_HISTORY.md`) — the west's
  full assembly→breakup→dispersal→reassembly story, the Western Ocean, and the
  O1/O3/O5 orogenies, all referenced above.
- **Paleoclimate** (`docs/PALEOCLIMATE.md`) — climate across the supercontinent
  cycle.
- **Physical Atlas** (`reports/regional/atlas/`) — 13 global plates that *cover*
  the Western Lands (relief, hypsometry, erosion, Köppen, temperature,
  precipitation, winds, **ocean currents**, **drainage basins**, NPP), plus
  present-day tectonic maps (`reports/tectonics/maps/present/`: elevation,
  plates, boundaries, features, margins).
- **20 regional gazetteers** (`reports/regional/regions/`) — but these are
  **icosahedral faces in a different coordinate frame**, not continents; none is
  labelled "Meridia" or "Selvana."

**Only on branch `claude/report-structure-analysis-wq3kga` (3 commits ahead of
`main`, fast-forwardable — *not yet landed*):**

- `docs/CONTINENTS.md` — the four-continent profile table.
- `docs/BIOGEOGRAPHY.md` + `reports/tectonics/biogeography_provinces.csv` — the
  per-continent habitat provinces cited here.
- `docs/SUBDUCTION_STYLE.md` + map — the Chilean/Mariana classification cited
  here.
- `reports/tectonics/continent_profiles.json` — the canonical per-continent
  numbers in this document.
- `tools/tectonics-pipeline/continents.yaml` + `lib/continents.py` — the naming
  and the shared connected-landmass assignment.
- New maps `present_biogeography.png`, `present_subduction_style.png`.

**The gap:** the regional (icosahedral) and continental (landmass) views are
**not cross-linked**, and there is **no continent-scoped report or map** — the
atlas is global, the gazetteers are triangular slices. This document is the first
continent-level synthesis; the assets to do more already exist.

Ordered by leverage:

1. **Land the continent-aware branch.** Fast-forward
   `claude/report-structure-analysis-wq3kga` so `CONTINENTS.md`,
   `BIOGEOGRAPHY.md`, `SUBDUCTION_STYLE.md`, the naming tooling, and the two new
   maps live on `main`. Everything below reads better once they do.

   continent(s) it covers (and by what fraction), turning the 20 gazetteers into
   a navigable continental atlas. This is the missing link between the two
   pipelines.
3. **Continent-clipped maps.** Render Meridia- and Selvana-centred views (relief,
   Köppen, drainage, biogeography) — today only global plates and per-face maps
   exist.
4. **Name the western features.** The O5 cordillera, the O3 Andean belt, the
   Western Ocean's T2 arc, and the major western rivers/basins carry codes but no
   place-names; a named-geography pass (tagged `INVENTED`, per convention) would
   give the Western Lands a real atlas vocabulary.
5. **A west-flank paleo-sequence.** Subset the existing global stage maps to the
   Meridia+Selvana split-and-reassembly, from the R3 rift to the projected boreal
   supercontinent.
